Bezier Tutorial

Bezier elements provide an alternative way to represent polynomial functions that provides a number of benefits to more traditional power series or Taylor series representations. Bezier elements are typically used in computer graphics and computer-aided geometry design applications due to their favorable mathematical properties and stable numerical evaluation.
  • When Bezier element is evaluated with parameters in the range of 01 the resulting point is guarenteed to be contained within the the polygon / polyhedron defined by the convex hull of its control points.
  • The deCasteljau algorithm provides a fast, recursive, means to evaluate the Bezier element that is very robust against numerical errors such as those caused by catastropic cancellation (due to finite floating point precision).

Definition

A Bezier element is essentially an pth order polynomial of n independent variables. Like all polynomaials, they can be expressed as a linear combinations of basis functions. For Bezier elements, the basis functions are the Bernstein polynomials .
